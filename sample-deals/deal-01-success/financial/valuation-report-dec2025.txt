BASIS OF VALUATION
------------------
Market Value as defined by RICS Valuation — Global Standards 2022 (Red Book).

PROPERTY DESCRIPTION
--------------------
Grade A multi-let office campus, four interconnected units.
Total NIA: 42,000 sq ft. Year built: 1998. Refurbished: 2018.
EPC Rating: B (44). BREEAM: Very Good (2018 certification).
Car parking: 180 spaces. Tenure: Freehold.

TENANCY AND INCOME
------------------
Fully let to four tenants on FRI leases.
Total passing rent: £1,083,000 p.a. (incl. parking supplement)
WAULT to expiry: 6.2 years. WAULT to break: 3.8 years.
No rent arrears. All leases in compliance.

VALUATION METHODOLOGY
---------------
The property has been valued using the Investment Method (term and reversion
approach) cross-checked against comparable transactions in the
Thames Valley / M4 Corridor office market (Q3/Q4 2025).

Comparable transactions reviewed:
- Apex Park, Theale (Oct 2025):  42,500 sq ft let estate — sold at 6.0% NIY
- Charter House, Newbury (Aug 2025): 28,000 sq ft — sold at 6.35% NIY
- Winnersh Triangle, Block D (Jun 2025): 55,000 sq ft — sold at 6.5% NIY

ASSUMPTIONS AND SPECIAL ASSUMPTIONS
-------------------------------------
1. All tenants are in occupation and paying rent.
2. No undisclosed onerous obligations.
3. The property is free of any contamination or hazardous substances.
4. All statutory consents for existing use are in place.
5. Title is good and marketable (subject to solicitors' confirmation).

MARKET COMMENTARY
-----------------
Thames Valley offices continue to see resilient demand from technology and
financial services occupiers. Grade A supply is constrained. Rents have
grown approximately 4–6% in 2024–2025 driven by flight to quality.
Investment volumes have recovered from H2 2024 lows, with prime yields
compressing slightly to 6.0–6.25% for well-let, long-WAULT assets.

VALUATION CONCLUSION
--------------------

Net Initial Yield (on passing rent £1,083,000): 5.86%
Reversionary Yield (on ERV £1,155,000): 6.25%
