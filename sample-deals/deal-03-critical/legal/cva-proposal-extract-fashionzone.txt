COMPANY OVERVIEW
-----------------
FashionZone Limited (Company No. 04987654) operates 14 high-street and
retail park fashion stores across the North West and Midlands. The company
employs 186 staff (124 FTE equivalent).

REASONS FOR CVA
----------------
Since 2022, FashionZone has experienced a sustained decline in revenues:
  - FY2022 revenue: £18.4m | EBITDA: £1.2m
  - FY2023 revenue: £15.8m | EBITDA: £0.3m (margin compression)
  - FY2024 revenue: £12.1m | EBITDA: -£0.8m (loss-making)
  - H1 FY2025 (Jul–Dec 2025): revenue £4.9m | EBITDA: -£1.1m (annualised)

The primary causes are:
1. Structural decline in high-street retail footfall (accelerated post-COVID)
2. Competition from online fast-fashion retailers
3. Above-market rents locked in during 2015–2017 lease cycle
4. Cost inflation (energy, minimum wage, business rates)

STORE PORTFOLIO — CVA TREATMENT
---------------------------------
The CVA proposes three categories for the company's 14 stores:

Category A — Continue (7 stores): Profitable or near-profitable.
  Full rent payable. No modification.

Category B — Modified terms (4 stores): Marginally viable.
  Rent reduced by 30% for CVA duration.
  *** PARKWAY RETAIL CENTRE (Unit 1, Stockport) IS A CATEGORY B STORE ***

Category C — Close (3 stores): Loss-making. Leases to be surrendered.
  Derelict stores in secondary locations.

PARKWAY RETAIL CENTRE — SPECIFIC TERMS
-----------------------------------------
Store                : Unit 1, Parkway Retail Centre, Stockport, SK1 3UA
NIA                  : 14,000 sq ft
Current rent (pre-CVA): £364,000 per annum (£26.00/sq ft)
CVA rent             : £254,800 per annum (£18.20/sq ft) — 30% reduction
CVA service charge   : £19,600 per annum (reduced from £28,000)
Pre-CVA arrears      : £62,300 (Nov 2025 – Jan 2026)
Deposit applied      : £91,000 (fully consumed against prior arrears)

Projected trading (Stockport store, CVA period Feb–Sep 2026):
  Revenue            : £960,000 (8 months)
  Cost of sales      : £576,000 (60% gross margin)
  Store costs (excl rent): £248,000
  CVA rent + SC      : £182,933 (8 months)
  Store contribution : -£46,933 (loss-making even at CVA rent)

  NOTE: The Stockport store is projected to be loss-making under CVA terms.
  FashionZone's Board has decided to continue trading to protect wider brand
  perception and because the lease expires September 2026 (no ongoing
  liability post-expiry).

CREDITOR TREATMENT
-------------------
Secured creditors   : Barclays Bank (floating charge) — paid in full from
                      CVA cash flows
Preferential        : HMRC — paid in full within 12 months
Unsecured creditors : 15p in the £ over 24 months
  - Landlord claims (rent arrears): £312,000 aggregate across all stores
  - Parkway Retail Fund claim: £62,300 → estimated recovery: £9,345
  - Trade creditors: £1.4m → estimated recovery: £210,000

SUPERVISOR'S ASSESSMENT
------------------------
The Proposed Supervisors believe the CVA offers a better outcome for
creditors than liquidation. In liquidation, unsecured creditors would
receive nil. Under the CVA, the dividend is estimated at 15p in the £.
